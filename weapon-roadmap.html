Phase 1 — The Daunt

Lv 3–7 · Uncommon Tier
Don't underestimate The Daunt — you can leave with 6–7 weapons covering most combat situations. The Prototype Spike Thrower from "A Bigger Boom" is the single best early pickup in the entire game. Buy the Sharpshot Bow from Chainscrape — you'll use it for hours.

Phase 2 — Into the Forbidden West (Post-Igniter)

Lv 7–17 · Rare Tier
After the Igniter ("Death's Door"), settlements like Plainsong, Scalding Spear, and the Carja Camp open up. Replace green weapons with blue (Rare) versions. The Hardweave Sharpshot Bow is your priority purchase — best early-game Sharpshot with a +25% Long-Range Damage perk.

Phase 3 — The Three Branches

Lv 17–24 · Very Rare Tier
The big leap. The Diving Mask unlocks Greenshine collection (required for upgrading purple/legendary weapons). The key weapon pickup is the Icestorm Boltblaster from Abadund — universally considered the best mid-game purchase in the entire game. After "The Kulrut," the Arena opens for legendary weapon grinding.
Must-Buy — Poseidon Branch (Sea of Sands)
VR
Icestorm Boltblaster Boltblaster
~1,437 shards + Bellowback Circulator + Large Machine Core. Sold by Abadund in Hidden Ember during The Sea of Sands. Fires Frost, Plasma, and Shock bolts — three elements from one weapon slot. "Remains a mainstay even into the later stages of the game." Must-Buy S-Tier

Greenshine Tip: With the Diving Mask, explore all 8 Sunken Caverns immediately — they contain Greenshine Slivers, Chunks, and Clusters. Greenshine is finite and non-respawning. Save it for the weapons you'll use endgame.
The High-Damage Combo: Apply Frost with a Blastsling → trigger Brittle status → hit with a fully charged Spike Thrower or Sharpshot Bow for massive amplified damage. For the largest machines, pin them with a Ropecaster first, freeze them, then unload.

Phase 4 — Mid-Game Exploration Window

Lv 20–30 · Legendary Grind
With all tools unlocked and Arena access, legendary weapons become available through activity chains. Arena medals are the most valuable currency — three S/A-tier legendaries sit behind Dukkah at 80 medals each. Start grinding now.
Arena Weapons (80 Medals Each from Dukkah)
L
Death-Seeker's Shadow Hunter Bow
Community #1 ranked weapon overall. Shock ammo, Targeting Hunter Arrows (guide overridden machines to attack), critical hit perks. The most versatile weapon in the base game. 80 Medals S-Tier
L
Forgefall Sharpshot Bow
Highest single-shot damage in the base game. Plasma sniper — charge shots devastate components. Strictly better than Regalla's Wrath. 80 Medals S-Tier
L
The Blast Forge Boltblaster
Armor-piercing DPS machine. Relentless sustained fire. Pairs well with frozen targets. 80 Medals A-Tier
Activity Chain Legendaries

L
Carja's Bane Warrior Bow
Complete all 4 Gauntlet Runs. Top-tier close-range DPS but the races can be frustrating. 4 Gauntlet Runs B-Tier
L

Full Stripes on all 12 Hunting Ground trials. Grindiest legendary to obtain, and Tripcasters remain the weakest weapon type. 12 Hunting Trials C-Tier

Phase 5 — Sunwing & Endgame

Lv 30–35+ · Final Legendaries
After unlocking the Sunwing during "GEMINI," the last legendary weapon becomes available. The Regalla fight in "Wings of the Ten" offers a weapon vs. armor choice — spare Regalla for the armor since Forgefall (Arena) outclasses Regalla's Wrath.
Late-Game Legendary
L
The Skykiller Spike Thrower
Side quest "The Way Home" at Legacy's Landfall (requires Sunwing). Talk to Harriem and Kristia on the ship. Explosive/Fire Spike Thrower. Pairs perfectly with Splitting Spike technique + Powershots Valor Surge. Free · Quest S-Tier
The Regalla Choice
VR
Regalla's Wrath Sharpshot Bow
Kill Regalla → get this Very Rare Sharpshot Bow. Strong, but Forgefall (Arena) is strictly better. The Tenakth High Marshal armor from sparing Regalla is more valuable since you can't get it any other way. Skip (spare Regalla)
Melee Pit Reward
⚔
+25% Spear Damage (permanent buff) Melee
Complete all 4 Melee Pits + defeat The Enduring. No weapon drop, but a permanent passive buff to your spear. Reward: Permanent Buff
No legendaries are missable. Every legendary weapon can be obtained in free-roam after the credits roll. The world stays fully open.

Upgrade System & Coil Guide

Reference
Workbench Upgrades — Each level costs Metal Shards + specific machine parts. Higher-rarity weapons require Greenshine (finite, non-respawning). Max levels: Green = 3, Blue = 4, Purple = 5, Legendary = 5. Upgrades unlock stat boosts, new ammo types, perks, and coil slots.
Coil Slots by Rarity
U
Uncommon Max 1 Coil
Upgrade freely — cheap materials. A fully upgraded green outperforms an un-upgraded blue.
R
Rare Max 2 Coils
Upgrade freely — still cheap. Will carry you through Phase 2–3.
VR
Very Rare Max 3 Coils
Requires Greenshine. Only upgrade the 3–4 purples you'll use long-term.
L
Legendary Start 3 → Max 5 Coils
Powerful immediately at base level. Full upgrades = 5 coil slots. Focus Greenshine here. Enable "Easy Loot" in custom difficulty to guarantee Apex machine part drops.
Best Coils (Priority Order)
①
Instant Proc Coils Instant Brittle / Corroding Chance
Trigger elemental states without elemental ammo. Game-changing on non-elemental weapons.
②
Component Tear +25% Tear Coil
Essential on Sharpshot Bows and Shredder Gauntlets for stripping machine parts (and loot).
③
Stealth Damage +25% Stealth Coil
Triggers with Smoke Bombs even mid-combat — not just from stealth grass. Huge damage multiplier.
④
Elite Overdraw Coils Burning Shores DLC
Hidden +50% knockdown bonus. DLC-exclusive but incredibly powerful on Sharpshot and Hunter Bows.
Core coiling philosophy: Stack identical coils to amplify a weapon's primary strength rather than mixing unrelated stats. Three of the same modifier always outperforms three different ones. Coils can only boost a weapon's existing capabilities — a Fire coil won't work on a weapon without fire ammo.

Community Weapon Type Tier List

Reference
Rankings synthesized from Reddit (r/horizon), GameRant, TheGamer, CBR, and community voting. This ranks weapon types overall. Your mileage may vary with playstyle.
S
Spike Throwers S-Tier
Highest burst damage in the game. Explosive/Fire spikes devastate large machines. The Skykiller + Splitting Spike + Powershots = the biggest damage numbers possible.
S
Shredder Gauntlets S-Tier
Catch-and-rethrow boomerangs that increase damage per catch. 4th throw triggers an explosion. Best Tear application in the game.
A
Sharpshot Bows A-Tier
Dominant single-target sniper damage. Forgefall's charged Plasma shots are devastating. Essential for weak point exploitation.
A
Blastslings A-Tier
Essential elemental setup tool. Frost → Brittle is the foundation of the game's highest damage combos. Every loadout should include one.
A
Hunter Bows A-Tier
Solid generalists carried by legendary variants. Fast fire rate, good ammo variety. Never the best at anything but good at everything.
B
Boltblasters B-Tier
High sustained DPS but enormous ammo consumption. The Icestorm (purple) is S-tier individually, but the type overall is ammo-hungry and slow to reload.
B
Warrior Bows B-Tier
Close-range rapid-fire. Excellent in melee builds (pair with Resonator Blasts). Falls off at range.
C
Ropecasters C-Tier
Utility tool for pinning large machines. Rarely worth a permanent wheel slot. No base-game legendary (DLC adds The Tie That Binds).
D
Tripcasters D-Tier
Community consensus: "sadly very underwhelming" vs Zero Dawn. Restrictive trap limits, clunky in fluid combat. Tinker's Pride is the hardest legendary to earn for the least payoff.
Based on community research from Reddit (r/horizon), GameFAQs, Game8, PowerPyx, TheGamer, CBR, and in-game data.
